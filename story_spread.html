The Right Thing to Say

Purple_peony

Summary:

Follow on of the fic ‘Say Yes’ from Aizawa’s perspective.

Hitoshi is seeing someone, or something like that. His adopted son seems to be keeping the details intentionally hazy, Aizawa has realised.
“You know who it is don’t you.” Yamada probes, can sense the gossip on his husband a mile away.
“I have an idea.”
“But you’re not gonna tell me are you? Meanie.” Yamada throws his shoulders back against the couch. Reaches his arm behind Aizawa’s neck, absently plays with his hair.
“It’s not our business.” Aizawa growls.
“Keep telling yourself that. You’ll step in eventually, I guarantee it.” Yamada’s fingers stray from the lock of Aizawa’s hair to the base of his skull. Gently massaging. Aizawa closes his eyes, leaning into the relief from a mild tension headache he hadn’t realised was there.
“I really won’t Hizashi.” He sighs.
“You really will.”

OR

Five times Aizawa doesn’t call his kid out for being a dumbass, and the one time he does.

Notes:

Not really a stand alone fic, ‘Say Yes’ is needed for context. I’ve added the chapters each section takes place in as markers so hopefully this all makes sense!

  1. Purple

Aizawa pulls open their front door with contained impatience. Hitohsi gives him a sheepish smile.

    “About time,” he sighs, and it is. He knows Hitoshi’s been back from his last undercover op for four days. Four days of one sentence answers to Yamada’s texts, four days worth of ignored calls. Aizawa tries very hard not to hold it against him. He understands better than most, recognises Hitoshi’s need for space after coming back from such an intense mission. This knowledge, however, had done nothing to ease the weighty anxiety pinned to his chest. Withdrawing as Yamada fretted. Desperate to see their kid, hyper aware of how any hint of pressure from them could send Hitoshi into a spiral. He’d come home when he was ready. Four days. The lingering hurt is rendered inert by the hug Hitoshi sweeps him into. He lets out a grunt that could be grudging acceptance but really is relief. Aizawa returns the hug. Catches a whiff of unfamiliar shampoo.

    “Where is she?” Hitoshi asks and Aizawa can feel him peering over his shoulder. Pulling back from the embrace and taking off his coat and shoes.

    “Enjoying her peace away from your harrassment.” Aizawa mutters as Hitoshi wanders further inside. Letting the warmth in his chest twitch his mouth into a small smile as Yamada rushes out and shares a ferocious hug with their son. The anxiety loosens it’s hold a little. Hiding his expression behind his hair, Aizawa watches his husband slowly rock Hitoshi in his arms. He holds the sight tightly, notices the way Hitoshi leans deeper into the hug. And that too is a relief. He’s well enough to be held, to seek comfort in them. Eventually Yamada releases him and Hitoshi slinks further into the house, hunting for his cat. Aizawa follows, watches the purple haired hero greet Spooky with enthusiasm which is quickly rebuffed.

    “Bitch cat,” Aizawa notes, slumping into one of the chairs at their dining table. Inspects Hitoshi as he fondly agrees and takes the seat opposite. He looks… well, better than he expected. Better than he hoped. Aizawa takes quick note of the energy in Hitoshi’s movements, the relaxed line of his shoulders. Swims through the conflicting swirls of pride and concern. Wonders if his son has any new scars underneath his clothing. Dark jeans and a band tee, a more put together outfit than expected too. 

    “How was the mission?” He probes quietly.

    “A delight.” Hisoshi responds lightly, quickly. An obvious deflection that Aizawa expected. Fixes his son with a hard stare, taking note of the greyish pallor to Hitoshi’s skin. The weight he’s lost around his face. Hitoshi knows he’s watching him, Aizawa doesn’t care. Lets the pressure of his gaze settle to see if anything comes loose. An inelegant interrogation but a sound one. Hitoshi tries to coax Spooky back into the room. Pointedly ignoring his fathers obvious appraisal. It’s mildly bizarre. Aizawa registers. Hitoshi’s hair is washed, clothes not only mildly coordinated but clean. He’s seen Hitoshi come off easier missions looking worse. The bags under his eyes are less pronounced than Aizawa thought they’d be. He looks okay, but not okay enough to tackle the task of laundry and personal care. Simple yet monumental tasks at this point. Aizawa knows, he’s been there. Someone has done it for him, Aizawa realises. Remembering the smell of shampoo from their hug earlier. An unfamiliar brand. Aizawa sits with the thought for a little longer. Hitoshi has slept, probably even eaten given his energy. He wonders where he’s been, who has… no. He drops the thought. It’s not his business. He won't pry. Hitoshi is here. Home. Looks well enough to pass for now. That’s enough. 

    “Hizashi made udon.” Aizawa says. Means you’re going to stay until I see you eat. Hitoshi gives him a brief smile, dulled but there. Aizawa lifts his gaze, wants Hitoshi to relax. 

    “That sounds nice.” Hitoshi murmurs before disappearing out of the room to annoy Spooky some more. After a few minutes Yamada wanders into the room, gives Aizawa a questioning look that he answers with a soft nod. Yamada relaxes, green eyes crinkling with a warm smile. He stoops to plant a brief kiss on Aizawa’s forehead.

    “Good to have him home.” Yamada says quietly. He can be quiet in moments like this. Aizawa reaches out, gives his husbands hand a squeeze. Agrees. 

  1. Red

Aizawa frowns down at his marking, trying not to notice the way Hitoshi is smiling at his phone. Obvious. It’s nice. He doesn’t let the swell of contentment show. A simple afternoon where they’re all home. Yamada is singing in the kitchen, Hitoshi sprawled over their couch. Aizawa lets himself lean into the peace of it, at ease. Hitoshi is recovering quicker than he anticipated. He knows the details of his last mission. Knows how messy it was, really. Hitoshi has a harder time controlling his empathic streak. The dread that had filled Aizawa’s stomach when he heard his son had started engaging with the mark intimately had almost made him sick with worry. Knows the deeper he goes the harder it is to return. But he has, returned. Mostly. Still skittish at times, a background insecurity that flares up when the situation is right. Like hay fever, or a past injury that acts up in cold weather. Cyclical. He had wondered if they would go away as Hitoshi got older. These paternal feelings. But they haven’t. Hitoshi had been a fan, then a protege. Aizawa remembers the flare of protective rage he experienced when he realised Hitoshi’s situation in foster care. Adopting him had been natural. The love he has for his son already in place by that point. A love that had come to him with surprising ease. Aizawa is used to working hard at love. Or perhaps grappling with the feeling that he himself is a hard person to love, or is undeserving of it. It had been easier for him to love as a parent, where that love came with service. Something with duty, conditions, consequence. More akin to a hero's responsibility to protect. The love he had received from his son in return had surprised him, it probably shouldn’t have but it did. The nervous gratitude, trust. He was a space of safety in Hiotshi’s eyes. And that was truly precious to him.  

Hitoshi taps at the keypad of his phone, smirks. The warm afternoon light lazy and golden. Aizawa relaxes into his armchair. Broody. Yamada would call him, like the blond isn’t equally as soft when it comes to their kid. Really, people shouldn’t be surprised that Aizawa is ultimately predisposed to nurture. He’s a teacher, quietly known for interfering more than strictly allowed when his students' wellbeing is at risk. Sure, he’s also harsh on them. Strict. But what’s the alternative? They die? Aizawa winces internally. The low hanging sun shifts the room into shadow. So maybe that feeling comes from a place of fear. Again, no one can blame him for that. From the corner of his eye he sees Hitoshi startle, someone is phoning him. Aizawa can hardly contain his surprise when Hitoshi actually answers. Notorious for blowing off calls. Aizawa turns his attention back to his papers. Still catches the confusion on his son's face. Ending the call after a few seconds then— phoning back?

    “Hey, sorry to call like this, can I talk to you for a minute? Are you alone right now?” Hitoshi says into the receiver with a tone that is far too serious for the lazy afternoon he’s having. Aizawa hears whoever it is on the other end of the line snap, Hitoshi holding up the pretence of urgency until: 

    “Oh nothing.” Hitoshi chirps, “you pocket dialled me a minute ago, sounded tense, so I thought I could give you an out.” Manipulative little shit. Aizawa keeps his focus on his papers, the person on the other end of the line doesn’t seem impressed either. Hitoshi flinches a little under the weight of the sudden silence.

    “Bakugou?” Hitoshi squeaks. Bakugou? He wouldn’t have called that one. What his kid is doing offering personal favours to UAs most explosive ex-student is a little beyond him. Aizawa tries to ignore the rest of their conversation, he’s being nosy. Yamada would call him a gossip but it’s hardly his fault Hitoshi is accepting what sounds like an invitation to Bakugou’s home right next to him. He’s observant is all. He’s trained to be, that’s not his fault. Hitoshi ends the call, flirtatiously.

    “Not staying for dinner?” Aizawa can’t help but inquire, gaze fixed on his marking. Listens to Hitosh make a show of telling their cat Peaches he’s got a date. 

    “Anyone Peaches should know about?” Aizawa mutters, catches the playful smirk Hitoshi shoots his way.

“Dunno, It’s a weird situation with a weird dude.” He explains searching the couch cushions for his belongings. 

    “Be careful.” Aizawa mutters, scowling at himself for poking into his business. 

    “I won’t get pregnant Dad.” Shinsou teases and it’s nice, to hear him joke and tease and emotionally regulate like a functioning human. Aizawa suppresses his smile, tries not to let his amusement show. 

  1. Yellow (Part 1)

There are leftovers in Hitoshi’s fridge. A lot of leftovers. More food than he’s seen in his son’s house, ever. Aizawa very, very pointedly ignores the neatly stacked containers of vegetable laden meals and grabs the milk (in date he notices). Hitoshi, seemingly oblivious to Aizawa’s internal confusion, pours coffee for them both. Aizawa adds his milk, shuffles back to the fridge. This time notices a banoffee pie, half eaten in the chaos. 

    “You’ve been busy.” He comments, voice neutral. Hitoshi looks up from his seat at the kitchen counter. Files littering the benchtop. Frowns. 

    “Just the usual.” He shrugs. Aizawa opens the fridge wider so Hitoshi can see the shelves full to bursting. Keeps his face achingly blank.

    “Oh,” Hitoshi realises what his father is referring to, runs a hand over the back of his neck. “A friend had leftovers to spare.” He elaborates quietly, shrugs again. Aizawa closes the fridge. Drops it. He’s here for work afterall. They spend a few hours pouring over case files and beefing up reports. The work is laborious, high detail and incredibly dull. The small intricacies of a large scale job, pulling strings that they may never see the full impact of. Still, both Aizawa and Hitoshi are meticulous. Aizawa watches his kid throw himself into the work, an unglamourous aspect of his dream job. Still facing it with that burning intensity Aizawa had admired in him when Hitoshi was 15. It’s pride, he’s proud of him. That’s allowed. Aizawa reminds himself. Watches Hitoshi’s hair get more and more unruly with the passing hours. Drain mug after mug of coffee. Absently tends to Spooky who periodically decides she requires attention more than the work they’re trying to get done. Yowling at them indignantly when she feels she’s being ignored. Hitoshi fusses over her, spends most of the afternoon gently moving her from the papers they need to go over. He won’t kick her out. As annoying as her interference is at times he never neglects to give her the attention she demands. Aizawa doesn’t even bother pretending that their relationship doesn’t make him smile. He’s a cat lover himself after all. 

    “Time for a break.” Aizawa finally grunts, they’re both overworked and finally the parental instinct wouldn’t let itself be ignored. Hitoshi gives him a distracted look.

    “There’s still some—” 

    “Time for a break. Eat something.” Aizawa won’t take no for an answer. He’s being a hypocrite, notorious for neglecting himself when he works like this. But there’s also something distinctly parental about that too. He wanders over to Hitoshi’s fridge and pulls out two random containers of neatly packaged food. Hitoshi relents, clearing spaces on the benchtop. Takes the food and a glass of water from his father. Rubs his face, notices how dry his eyes are. The food is good. A spinach and something quiche and a salad with some unfamiliar grain. Aizawa eats slowly. Bakugou had always been a good cook. Aizawa remembers him quietly feeding his friends leftovers in the dorms. Always happening to make someone's favourite if they were having a hard time. An unexpected nurturing side that Aizawa was very careful not to outwardly notice. The food is good. It leaves a cold feeling in the pit of his stomach. If Hitoshi… if Hitoshi wasn’t doing well, if Bakugou noticed, he’d probably cook for him. Aizawa is fairly certain the food is from him. The neat portions and well balanced serves scream Bakugou. A control freak at heart. He eyes Hitoshi quietly. His mental health had been improving steadily over the past few months. He’s gained back some muscle and body weight, his apartment regularly cleaned and smells fresh. Has he missed something? Progress is not fixed, relapses happen without cause. And as much as they’re close, Hitoshi has a bad habit of hiding what hurts. Always has. Aizawa grimaces, remembers Hitoshi’s stifled tears, fears stuffed tightly in the back of his throat. Unable to ask for his help. 

    “Are you alright?” He asks, puts down his food. He’s worrying, over nothing probably. Hitoshi gives him a questioning look.

    “Yeah, of course.” He tilts his head, a question.

    “Your friend made you a lot of food. Maybe they realised you haven’t been eating.” Aizawa lets himself ask without asking. A habit that infuriates Yamada at times, in the past few years Hitoshi has started to notice it too. 

    “That’s not it Dad, I’m fine.” Hitoshi smiles. To Aizawa it seems genuine. He doesn’t respond, after a few beats Hitoshi fills the silence. 

    “They were having a bad day, cooking distracts them so they came over. Must have realised I’m an eater.” An answer, not the one Aizawa had been expecting, but an answer. He’s happy to let it drop. As long as Hitoshi is fine, but, he seems to have something else to add.

    “You’re a good friend then, if they came to you.” Aizawa prompts. Watches a flush of colour pass over Hitoshi’s cheeks. He fiddles with his food, flustered.

    “Hardly,” he manages, “I just hung out and ate food.” He runs his hand over the back of his neck. Aizawa doubts that’s all there was to it but he lets it slide.

    “As long as you’re not taking too much on.” Aizawa warns, even though this relationship, he titles it vaguely, seems to be doing the opposite of hinder Hitoshi’s recovery. 

    “It’s not like that.” Hitoshi assures quickly. “They just needed somewhere to come, they’re other friends were probably busy.” Or they just wanted to come here. Aizawa adds internally. 

    “Sure.” He agrees, unconvinced. Waits as Hitoshi chews over whatever he’s thinking.

    “I’m not exactly the person people think to come to for emotional support Dad.” He ends up deciding on. 

    “Yet they came to you.” Aizawa leaves it at that, distracts them both with the remaining work they have to get done. Ends up leaving with two massive slices of banoffee pie for him and Yamada (or more likely Yamada and Yamada). Hitoshi hugs him in the entryway, a real hug that lasts a good few seconds. Aizawa weathers it, pretends not to notice the hitch in his heart. 

Remembers back to the first time Hitoshi hugged him, a few months after he and Yamada had started fostering him. Over that time Aizawa had patiently watched Hitoshi lean into their gentle attempts at physical comfort. Wary and aloof for the most part until one evening Hitoshi had broken something, a mug if he remembers rightly? He had offered to make tea? Anyway he dropped it and it smashed. An accident, no harm. Aizawa had gone into the kitchen to investigate the noise to see his foster child picking up the pieces of broken ceramic in his socks. Almost chastised him for it before he noticed Hitoshi’s hands were shaking. Flinching when he noticed Aizawa in the doorway. Mouth pressed together tightly, eyes glazed with panicked tears.

“Hitoshi.” Aizawa had become used to using his given name by then. Hitoshi glanced down at the mess, dropping the piece of broken mug in his hands. 

    “It’s alright, we’ll clean it.” Aizawa had assured. And he could see Hitoshi didn’t believe him, that it was alright. He shook his head, a nonverbal apology. A sickening habit Aizawa had noticed, Hitoshi’s inability to speak when he got upset. Like he had been taught to shut himself down in moments like this. Aizawa had acted then, stepping into the room he placed a hand on Hitoshi’s shoulder. Hoping it was grounding for his foster son. Felt the irregularity of Hitoshi’s breathing, the tension in his shoulders. Shutting himself up tight, face screwed into a grimace. Desperately trying to squash his features into a mask of neutrality. Hands shaking. When the touch wasn’t shaken off he simply, picked Hitoshi up. Lifted him off the ground and carried him out of the kitchen.

    “You’ll cut your feet.” Aizawa had explained simply as Hitoshi clung awkwardly to his shoulders. Scrambling for purchase. But Aizawa had him in a strong hold, secure. Reminiscent of a parent carrying a young child. Something Aizawa never got to do for him before, but could proxy now. 

    “As long as you’re not hurt it’s fine.” He had told him, gently placing Hitoshi down out of harm's way. Keeping one hand on his shoulder. Aizawa was still taller than his son then. Hitoshi was looking down, and Aizawa watched his lip tremble. 

    “I’m sorry Sensei,” he was still Sensei then. Dad wouldn’t come until the adoption papers were signed months later. He still felt like Dad regardless. 

    “I’m not angry Hitoshi, it was an accident.” Aizawa had kept his voice as soft as he could. Waited as Hitoshi sank inwards, shaking his head, mouth pulled down. Face reddening with tears he refused to release.

    “Nah,” he had mumbled, “shouldn’t be breaking your things Sensei I—”

    “It’s alright Hitoshi,” he forced the purple haired teen to look at him then, squeezing his shoulder. Worried that the physical contact might be too much but unwilling to let his foster son go. Hitoshi’s eyes were cloudy with anxiety, searching Aizawa’s face for anger. Flushed with panic.

    “Sorry,” Hitoshi managed, voice tight, stretched thin. He looked away, blinking hot tears from his eyes.

    “It’s alright, Hitoshi,” Aizawa repeated, meant I’m alright, I’m not like whatever you’re imagining. He had waited, gently rubbing his thumb over Hitoshi’s shoulder. Watched his foster child try to squeeze down the flood of emotions. Struggle to control his breathing. 

    “I can see that you’re upset, that’s alright but you don’t have to be. I’m not mad, it was an accident.” Aizawa had assured again, hoping it was the right thing to say. Willing Hitoshi to believe him. Hoped that they had built up enough trust for the assurance to land. Felt Hitoshi start to pull away from him, the touch, then stutter back towards him, trembling under his hand. 

    “Sorry Sensei, I’m— I’m overreacting, sorry—” Hitoshi’s voice had come out scratchy and hollow. The sound sending chills through Aizawa’s chest. You’re not. He had known, with a dread certainty. I know this is not just about the mug.

    “Hitoshi,” he had whispered, unable to articulate it in a way Hitoshi would accept. Recognised the shame in Hitoshi’s posture. Fidgeting, shaking his head, trying to pull himself together. Breaking at the seams. Aizawa squeezed his shoulder again. 

    “I’m here.” Take comfort in me. Aizawa checked his own vulnerability, the desperate desire to help his kid. Help that he could only give if Hitoshi let him. So he waited, not pushing but never leaving. Until finally Hitoshi reached out, grasped the fabric of Aizawa’s shirt. So tightly. Shaking from the strain of keeping so much inside himself. Searching Aizawa’s face he said:

    “Are you sure it’s okay?” With real aching, uncertainty in his eyes.

Aizawa had nodded then, didn’t trust his own voice. Shifted forward a little closer. Then watched as Hitoshi took a gulp of air and pressed his forehead into Aizawa’s shoulder. Finally, letting out a shaky exhale. Tense movements that eventually became an embrace. Who on Earth hurt you like this? Aizawa had wondered, equal parts enraged and relieved. Angered by whoever or whatever had made a bright, talented, kind kid like Hitoshi cower this way. Happy that at least he— he could be the person Hitoshi came to for comfort. He hugged Hitoshi tighter. Pretended not to notice the hitching breaths that was his son crying. 

    “You gonna let go of me old man?” Hitoshi’s voice startles Aizawa out of his thoughts. Pulling back Aizawa shifts the bag of food in his hand. Hitoshi gives him a look, familiar, cheeky. And for a moment Aizawa sees how his face has changed with age, registers how much of a man he has become. 

    “I’m proud of you.” He says simply, watching the warm surprise spread across Hitoshi’s features. 

    “Where did that come from?” Hitoshi half laughs.

    “Nowhere, just,” Aizawa looks over at his son, registering another swell of pride. “Just eat the food in your fridge before it goes bad.” 

  1. Yellow (Part 2)

Aizawa usually grumbles at the frequency Yamada coerces Hitoshi over for dinner. Insists their kid has his own life, better things to do than hang out with two middle aged teachers. Yamada lets him, knows he looks forward to Hitoshi’s visits as much as he does. And Aizawa will admit, he likes it. Likes that even as a grown man Hitoshi still seems to enjoy spending time with his parents. At first he had put it down to lost time. Fifteen years to make up for. However now he suspects, and hopes, that Hitoshi just likes them. Likes coming home. Seems to feel comfortable explaining his boy trouble over a meal. Yamada has been mulling it over since Hitoshi left an hour ago. Keeps finding things to clean, rearrange. A restless excitement despite their kids rather... oblivious take on the situation. Hitoshi has feelings for this person, Bakugou, Aizawa suspects. From what Hitoshi said they’ve been seeing each other casually for a few months now. The limits of their relationship left intentionally hazy. Another of Hitoshi’s habits, leaving well enough alone, or perhaps, another instance of his son avoiding emotional confrontation. It had been painfully obvious Hitoshi is interested in more than friendship, or friendship and sex. So obvious in fact it seemed almost intentional. And maybe it was. An insular ignorance that shielded Hitoshi from realising any deeper feelings. Maybe he’s not ready for that. Aizawa can’t blame him. Relates to that even. Yamada pulls a random utensil Aizawa has never seen either of them use before from a kitchen drawer. Can practically hear the does this spark joy? Pop into Yamada’s head. What the fuck even is that? An egg whisk? A mummification tool? Yamada sets it down on the table top, digs around the drawer to, no doubt, pull out more useless junk.

    “Stop that.” Aizawa mutters, Yamada gives him a look from the kitchen, brow raised. “You’re fussing, just come sit down. Let him figure it out at his own pace.” He chastises. Yamada lets out an exasperated sigh. Shuffles into the lounge to sit beside his husband on their couch, shoots him a pout. Tosses his long blond hair over his shoulder. 

    “He likes them though!” Yamada whines. “Really likes them, it’s like you don’t want to see him happy.” Yamada teases. Aizawa gives him a flat look.

    “He’s healing Zashi, don’t push.” 

    “But he’s—” 

    “He’s not there yet.” Aizawa ignores the frustrated frown his husband gives him. Green eyes peeking over the rims of his glasses, they’ve slipped down his nose. Aizawa wants to fix them. Now he’s the one fussing. 

    “And people say I baby him.”

    “I don’t—” Aizawa breaks off, immediately too defensive, catches Yamada’s grin. “He’s smart.” He says instead, leans back on the sofa to turn his body towards Yamada more. “It’s not our business.”

    “He’s our kid.” Yamada insists. “I’m so proud of him, so so so proud of him. He deserves love babe, I think he’s ready for it.” Yamada smiles, scooches closer to Aizawa. Their thighs brushing. 

    “You’re a sap.”

    “I’m a romantic.” Yamada dismisses, “and so are you, and so is he.” He reminds. Resting a hand on Aizawas knee. Rubs his thumb in gentle circles as Aizawa frowns to himself. Blinks his dry eyes. It’s more complicated than that and Yamada knows it.

    “He’s a bleeding heart Zashi,” Aizawa mutters eventually. “You know what he’s like. I don’t want someone taking advantage of that.”

    “Normally I’d agree.” Yamada considers. “But this new person seems to be genuine.”

    “Anyone can seem that way.”

    “Stop sucking the joy out of it Shou!” Yamada chastises, pinching Aizawa’s thigh. Gives his husband a stern look. “If someone has gotten this close to him, which is not actually that easy babe, they must be something.” Yamada decides. Aizawa gives a grudging hum of agreement. “You know who it is don’t you.”

    “I have an idea.”
    “But you’re not gonna tell me are you? Meanie.” Yamada throws his shoulders back against the couch. Reaches his arm behind Aizawa’s neck, absently plays with his hair.

    “It’s not our business.” Aizawa growls.

    “Keep telling yourself that. You’ll step in eventually, I guarantee it.” Yamada’s fingers stray from the lock of Aizawa’s hair to the base of his skull. Gently massaging. Aizawa closes his eyes, leaning into the relief from a mild tension headache he hadn’t realised was there. 

    “I really won’t Hizashi.” He sighs.

    “You really will.”

  1. White

He really tries. He tries so hard. 

    “Switchblade,” he mutters darkly into his earpiece, “this is obviously a personal conversation. Please disconnect the other agents, I’ll let you know if anything work related happens.” He hears a muffled sigh come through the line.

    “Yes Eraser,” comes the dejected ascent.

    “Sleeping with the enemy isn’t a fucking deal breaker, I’m supposed to to whatever it takes to get close to the mark.” Hitoshi’s voice sounds through Katsuki’s earpiece, clear as day. Aizawa sighs. He tries. He really does. Tries enough not to interject when he hears Katsuki curse, and use Hitoshi’s given name. Tries enough to keep his mouth shut when he hears Hitoshi swear and yell. A dark anger in his tone that makes Aizawa’s stomach sink. Wonders if he should intervene but whatever communication is happening now is more than whatever they’ve been doing the past four months. Even if it is ugly. Tries to zone out of the conversation enough to give them some semblance of privacy while simultaneously checking for any sign of villain interaction. He grinds his teeth as he listens to his kid being stubborn, self-critical and dramatic. Revealing fears about himself that has Aizawa cursing himself for not doing more to prevent. 

    “You’re not a villain.” He hears Katsuki snap. An inelegant delivery but he agrees with the sentiment.

    “I was at the time, for a moment, a minute, an hour, I hurt people and I didn’t give a shit.” 

Hitoshi, Aizawa squeezes his hands into fists around his capture weapon. Feels a low anger at himself begin to burn in his stomach. It hurts. Hurts to hear the person he’s tried so hard to nurture harbour such honest self loathing. He almost says something, but the conversation moves on and Katsuki seems to be arguing their case well enough on his own. 

    “I know that you’re not a fucking villain.” 

    “How the fuck can you be so sure?” Aizawa’s stomach drops at the sound of Hitoshi’s voice breaking.

    “Because you had an anxiety attack while fingering my ass!” 

Aizawa blinks, thinks for a moment that he’s misheard. But the shocked laughter that follows cements the words into place. Suddenly he’s engulfed in waves of second hand embarrassment. Shakes his head. These kids will be the death of me. He thinks, not for the first time. Presses his palm into his forehead as he listens to the most awkward confession he’s ever heard. Bites his tongue. It’s not like he can judge. Years of desperate self-sabotaging and pining after Hizashi prickle at the back of his mind. They’re idiots but he, he had also been an idiot. He reminds himself.  Flattening his affection with bland indifference that, now he looks back, fooled no one . Intentionally misunderstanding Hizashi’s very loud, very raw confession after they graduated because he was… well, he doesn’t need to dwell on that now. Until years later, years of walking around with a broken heart. An intentionally broken heart that he kept breaking himself. Every time Hizashi would linger, check in on him, offer that wide smile and open kindness. Until he caved, exhausted. Half dead from another mission, folded in Hizashi’s arms as they sat together on his sofa. Drained, feeling the ache of it in his bones. Hizashi, and the tender way he stroked his hair and his endless stupid patience for him, Shouta Aizawa. There had been a lull in the show they were pretending to watch and he said I love you through gritted teeth like it hurt. And from what he remembers it did. 

    “Baby if we were dating, if we were dating you’d fucking know. I’d treat the shit out of you oh my God, if we were dating…” He hears Hitoshi trail off, elated.

    “If?” Katsuki’s voice echoes down the line and enough is enough.

    “Yeah if?” Aizawa grunts into his earpiece, listens with perhaps a little too much satisfaction at the panicked silence that follows.

    “Bakugou did you… you did mute your earpiece, right?” Hitoshi says, voiced pitched higher with desperation and Aizawa grins at the crumpled thwack of Katsuki grasping at his earpiece.   

“Oh my God.” 

     And 1 — White (Part 2)

Aizawa falls into step beside his son as they make their way to the train station. Can feel the city stirring around them as dawn starts to creep up on the sky. It had been a draining night. Not only has Hitoshi survived a villain attack, successfully talking her into surrendering with nothing more than a few scratches, the purple haired hero had also stayed with her through every step of her processing at the station. Aizawa registers the exhaustion in Hitoshi’s posture. Shoulders drooping, head hanging low. Maybe it’s not just exhaustion. 

    “Bakugou waited for you outside the station.” He comments lightly, tests the waters. Hitoshi sinks a little further. 

    “It doesn’t matter, think I ruined it.” Hitoshi gives a nonchalant shrug, expression tight. 

    “Did you speak to him?” Aizawa asks, wonders if he should drop it. But the thinly disguised pain on his son's face has him forcing the issue.

    “Yeah,” Hitoshi nods, keeps his voice light. “Don’t worry Dad I apologised to the other kid. You won’t have any angry parents phoning you.” He jokes, shooting Aizawa a smirk that drops from his face too quickly to be genuine.

    “You’re upset.” Aizawa says, too tired to be subtle. Hitoshi’s face darkens. 

    “It’s fine Dad.” It’s very obviously not. Aizawa lets them sit in uncomfortable silence. Apparently unbothered by the tension, shuffling through the desolate station and taking a seat to wait for the first train. He watches Hitoshi buy a can of coffee he absolutely should not be drinking right now then slouch into the seat beside him. Still doesn’t say a word. 

    “You’re upset.” Aizawa says again, Hitoshi glares. Picks at the tab of his coffee can.

    “I did the right thing.” Hitoshi says, flat, stubborn. Cracking open his drink and taking a swig. Grimaces. At himself or the disgusting drink Aizawa can’t tell. “I did my job and I pulled it off,” he folds his arms. “Manipulation is a part of that. It sucks I had to lie to him but it was the best outcome.” 

    “Doesn’t mean you’re not upset—” 

    “Jesus Dad it’s fine.” Hitoshi snaps. Aizawa raises a brow. Waits as Hitoshi shakes his head at himself, sighs. Nail tapping against the can in his hands. His overtired frustration in morse code. “I fucked it up, okay? He liked me and I fucked it up.” He mutters. Aizawa shifts his feet. 

    “Yes…I heard.” Aizawa casts him a side eye, Hitoshi makes a pained sound.

    “How many people heard?”

    “Just me, turned the other mics off.”

    “Thank fuck.” Hitoshi let’s out a sigh, rubbing the back of his neck. “At lest I can let him know he only has to murder you now.” Hitoshi scrubs a hand over his face. “What do I have to do to ensure you never speak of it again? To anyone. Including Papa.”

    “Talk to him.” Aizawa’s voice is as deadpan as usual, Hitoshi gives him a wide eyed look.

    “Why? I told you I fucked it up.”

    “I thought you did the right thing? You can’t have both Hitoshi.” Aizawa reasons, watches the drained frustration set into Hitoshi’s features. He downs another gulp of coffee. 

    “Yeah?” Hitoshi struggles, “well… I contain multitudes.” He mutters, leans his arms against his thighs.

    “Cute, but changes nothing.” 

    “Ugh,” the tapping resumes. Aizawa wishes he would just throw the empty caffeine away.

    “You have feelings for him.”

    “Stop asking me questions with assumptions.”

    “It’s not a question. It’s been obvious for weeks.” Aizawa gives Hitoshi a hard look, watches him slouch in on himself even further.

    “I do. But it’s over.”

    “Enough Hitoshi.” Aizawa growls, causing his son to startle and look over at him. He’s tired of watching his kid feel shit about himself. “I handle teenagers everyday, don't make me do it now.” He sighs, purple eyes search his face. “If he doesn’t reach out in a few days, call him yourself. If he does contact you, talk to him. This way,” Aizawa says, flat, serious, “Hizashi will never find out.”

    “That’s a childish threat.”

    “Then stop acting like a child.” Aizawa growls without much venom. He’s tired. Hitoshi pouts, scuffs his boot against the pavement. Aizawa sighs.

    “Zashi thinks…” Aizawa trails off, giving Hitoshi a resigned look. “Thinks you’re ready for it. And,” he gives Hitoshi a hard stare, “so do I. You have…” Aizawa lets the pride swell to the surface. You have grown so much, can’t you see how far you’ve come? “He makes you happy,” he says, letting the softness mellow his voice. “You deserve that, whether you believe that or not it’s true.” He mutters, blinking his dry eyes. 

    “Dad.” Hitoshi croaks.

    “And,” Aizawa forces eye contact, “what you said last night, you’re not a villain, you’re my kid.”

    “I’m a mess.” Hitoshi laughs, throat tight.

    “You’re my kid.” Aizawa says again, isn’t exactly sure what he means by it but something inside Hitoshi seems to relax. “You’re my kid.” He says again, softly.

    “Thanks Dad.”

    “Just talk to him.” Aizawa grumbles, looks down the track, hears the train distantly.

    “Okay.” Hitoshi murmurs, looking down at his hands. “I agree to your terms.”

    “Good.” Says Aizawa gruffly. “Then Hizashi will never know a single thing I overheard last night.” Hitoshi groans again.

    “Dad I’m begging you—”

    “It happened the night before you came to see us, right?” Aizawa hides his grin as Hitoshi’s face heats up. Giving his answer away. Aizawa makes a satisfied sound.

    “I did wonder who washed your hair.”

Prologue/Silver

    “I have a system Shou, you put mugs on the bottom rack, just let me—” Hizashi insists, Shouta tries to intervene with stacking the dishwasher anyway.

    “Keep the door open!” Hizashi calls down the hall, hears Hitoshi respond, then the door to his old bedroom slam closed. Shouta pulls the sleeves of his sweater up over his forearms, starts just rinsing and handing the dirty dishes to Hizashi who stacks them neatly in the dishwasher. Can hear Hizashi humming over the noise from the sink. Quickly tidying the last of the plates for his husband he sets about making tea. They were both a little surprised when Hitoshi asked if he could bring Katsuki for dinner on Christmas. As much as they love their kid they’re both aware he has a habit of… letting things sit, taking things slowly. More out of anxiety than apathy but this time he seems to be embracing the change. Pushing his comfort zone to allow Katsuki into his life more and more. 

    “Is the door closed, Shou can you check?” Hizashi gives him a playful nudge. Shouta rolls his eyes, pokes his head down the hall to see Hitoshi’s bedroom door firmly closed.

    “It’s shut.” He grunts, returning to the kitchen, again trying to reach the kettle.

    “Good, oh my God Shou, they’re so cute.” Hizashi yell whispers, grasps at his husband's arm.

    “Save it for when they go love, you’re still being noisy.” Shouta leans into him, Hizashi’s grin infectious. 

    “They’re so cute though babe.” Hizashi beams, sweeping Shouta into his arms. “Did you see Hitoshi? The way he was smiling?”

    “I saw.” 

    “And Bakugou, that’s Bakugou?” Hizashi sways them with his uncontained energy. Shouta smiles, brushes Hizashi’s long blond hair off his shoulders. “He’s grown up so well, I’m so proud of him. And he baked for us!” Hizashi continues in a mock whisper. Shouta chuckles, rubs his hand in circles against Hizashi’s lower back.

    “Sounds like you’ve taken the bribe.” He muses. Hizashi shoots him a grin.

    “How weird do you think it was for him hearing his old teacher say cock five times in a sentence?”

    “Pretty weird Hizashi,”

    “I hope so.” He giggles, Shouta lets out a huff of laughter. Pulling his husband closer to him. Hizashi smells like the woody notes of his cologne, hair slipping out of his messy bun. Shouta watches the way his eyes crinkle, well worn smile lines. It’s Christmas and he’s in a good mood. Spending it with his family.

    “Are you going to let me get started on tea?” He shifts his hold to Hizashi’s hips, feels the hands on his arms grip him tighter.

    “No,” Hizashi smirks, “it’s Christmas, I think you should romance me instead.” They decide. Shouta rolls his eyes. Sways Hizashi closer.

    “The kids might see.” He whispers, lets himself smile as Hizashi wraps his arms around Shouta’s shoulders.

    “What do you think they’re doing behind that closed door?” Hizashi reasons. Shouta gives him a hum of agreement before leaning forward and giving them a gentle kiss. Feeling Hizashi smile into it before they break apart.

    “What did Hitoshi ask you not to talk about when they first arrived?” Hizashi asks, simply, sweetly. Shouta knows it’s a trap, tries to push the blond away but Hizashi wraps his arms around him tighter.

    “Nothing.” Shouta lies, obviously. Struggles to pull away.

    “Babe please, you have to tell me.” Hizashi insists, refusing to give an inch.

    “I really can’t Zashi I promised, it was part of our deal.” Shouta tries, giving the blond a pleading look.

    “What deal?”

    “He bought my silence by… talking to Bakugou.” Shouta admits. Hizashi releases him, giving the black haired man an incredulous look.

    “You meddled!” He accuses, pointing a finger at his husband.

    “I—“ Shouta tries but is cut off by Hizashi’s laughter.

    “I told you.”

    “It wasn’t like that.”

    “It so was.”

    “Hizashi.”

    “You blackmailed our child into telling Bakugou how he feels. I called it. I called it!” Hizashi waves his arms so Shouta grabs him by the wrist. Pinning him against the kitchen counter.

    “That’s not—“

    “Don’t even try it.” Hizashi shakes his head, uncaring about being sandwiched between his spouse and the kitchen draws. “You are,” he grins, “the biggest softie—“

    “Hizashi,” Shouta presses their foreheads together, hoping to distract the other man with their sudden close contact. Hizashi grins into his face. “You’re being very loud dear.” Shouta chastises quietly, Hizashi’s eyes bright with mischief.

    “Maybe you can shut me up Eraser—”

    “Dad!” Hitoshi’s shocked voice startles them out of their embrace. Shouta quickly dropping his husband's arms and stepping back.

    “Inappropriate behaviour.” Hitoshi mock scolds, Shouta rolls his eyes. 

    “Sorry Toshi,” Hizashi straightens his apron with a smirk. “Your father just can’t keep his hands off me.” 

    “Now Katsuki knows I was raised in a house of debauchery.” Hitoshi laments.

    “Just don’t tell social services.” Shouta mutters, glancing at a very red looking Katsuki, and stomping over to the kettle to finally make some tea. Listening to the easy banter between Hitoshi and Hizashi, gives his family a mock sigh at their teasing. Watches the casual touches between his son and Bakugou, a growing intimacy. Hitoshi seems, relaxed isn’t quite the right word for it. Comfortable, content. An ease to his demeanor the Shouta puts down to the way Katsuki leans into him. He feels another ring of deep seeded anxiety loosen from his chest. His son is letting himself fall for another person. And Shouta is grateful all over again that as much as he and his son are alike, Hitoshi is stronger than he was at that age. Because Shouta, well, he keeps himself wound tight. As much as he knows he doesn’t need to now. Like the binding of his capture weapon, stiff fibres. Yamada has worked hard to unravel him in the years they’ve been together. Worked harder than he should have, Shouta knows this, guiltily. He still feels guilty about it. Tries very hard not to let those quiet shameful feelings sully the ground that Yamada has already fought so hard to gain. Hitoshi is braver than he is. Loving, his son is a loving person. Is letting that side of him take what it needs. And Shouta is hopeful, hopeful that perhaps he and Hizashi had something to do with that.
